Details of Raila-Obama meeting at the former prime minister's office

February 13, 2024
Post

- Visiting former US president Barack Obama has held talks with Raila

- Obama promised to help Raila and Uhuru implement their handshake deal

- The two leaders also discussed how to bring lasting peace in South Sudan

- Earlier, Obama met and held talks with Uhuru at State House

- Obama is expected to tour his ancestral home in K'Ogelo before proceeding to South Africa

Retired US President Barack Obama on Sunday, July 15, held talks with opposition leader Raila Odinga shortly after meeting President Uhuru Kenyatta at State House, Naiobi, upon his arrival for a two-day visit.

Obama, who was accompanied by his sister Auma Obama met the former prime minister and his daughter Winnie Odinga at his office in Nairobi.

Confirming the afternoon meeting, Raila said they talked about a number of issues, ranging from the March 9, 2018, handshake to the ongoing peace talks between President Salva Kiir and Riek Machar in South Sudan.

"It's always a great pleasure meeting with one of Kenya’s most famous sons, President Barack Obama. He spoke very passionately about his foundation and particularly the empowerment of the youth and I commend both him and his sister Auma Obama for the commendable job," Raila said in a Facebook post seen by TUKO.co.ke on Sunday.

The former PM noted Obama promised to support him and President Uhuru in the implementation of the Building Bridges initiative and also in the ongoing fight againts corruption.

"Obama threw his support behind the Building Bridges initiative and pledged his support in helping bring the country together and fighting corruption," he said.

As was expected, Obama landed at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) on Sunday afternoon and was welcomed by Uhuru.

The son of K'Ogelo is expected to tour his ancestral home in Siaya county on Monday after which he will proceed to South Africa where he will deliver the annual Nelson Mandela Lecture.
