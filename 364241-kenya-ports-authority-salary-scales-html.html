About Kenya Ports Authority

Kenya Ports Authority (KPA) was formulated under an Act of Parliament. Over time, and with the introduction of inland container depots at various localities, the parastatal's responsibilities have expanded beyond coastline ports.

The functions of Kenya Ports Authority are as follows:

  • Maintain sea and inland waterway ports in Kenya.
  • Operate all inland and watersports.
  • Improve and regulate all service areas in the country.

KPA is a semiautonomous government-owned entity that has been successfully managing port operations in the country. With the intent of modernization of all port facilities to expand cargo holding and processing capabilities, KPA has been hiring industry professionals to realize its objectives.
