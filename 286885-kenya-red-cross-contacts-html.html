Company description and services

Kenya Red Cross society can basically be defined as a voluntary organization which operates in a network of 8 regions and 64 branches through the nation. Red Cross has over seventy-thousand volunteers who assist in the implementation of various activities both at the headquarters and also in the branch levels. For those who would want to become members, they can do so anytime. Membership to Red Cross is open to everyone regardless of their gender, religion, race, class, political opinion or nationality.

Fundamental principles of Kenya Red Cross

1. Humanity

Being a piece of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement, the Kenya Red Cross is conceived of a longing to offer help without discriminating the individuals who require their assistance. They endeavor to mitigate and prevent human suffering both on a national and international capacity. Their main duty is to protect life and health of human and ensure respect for the human is given. They also endeavor to promote mutual gender, race, nationality and religion.

2. Neutrality

Because Kenya Red Cross wants to continue enjoying the confidence of all, this movement does not take sides in case hostile situations arise. They don’t engage in controversies of a racial, political, religious, or ideological nature at any time during their service provision.

3. Impartiality

The Red Cross society does not discriminate against people depending on their religious believes, class, political opinion, nationality or race. They endeavour to relieve human suffering and are solely guided by the people’s needs. They always give priority to urgent cases of distress but still manage to help those who are not facing emergency cases.

4. Independence

This movement is independent. Even though National Societies are auxiliaries in humanitarian service provision of their governments, they are subjects to the laws and regulations of their respects countries. Red Cross Kenya maintains this autonomy so that they can act according to the principles of their movement.
