Oscar Sudi says Jubilee Party should discipline Uhuru for inviting strangers

Kapseret MP Oscar Sudi has yet again lashed out at President Uhuru Kenyatta saying he should be disciplined by the ruling Jubilee Party for inviting "strangers" to the outfit.

Sudi's angry remarks came after it emerged the Tanga Tanga lawmaker had been earmarked as one the six legislators set to be recalled for allegedly disrespecting their party leader.

Sudi termed Uhuru's imminent decision to expel him as laughable as he wondered why he was going for "political rejects" at the expense of members who formed the party.

"This is totally laughable. Uhuru is dumping his loyal supporters and going for political rejects in KANU who are less than two in numbers. The president himself deserves a disciplinary action from the party since he invited strangers to our party contrary to wishes of our party members,"he argued.

Besides, Sudi others set to face the axe in the ongoing Jubilee purge are Soy lawmaker Caleb Kositany, Kimani Ngunjiri (Bahati), Rigathi Gachagua (Mathira), Alice Wahome of Kandara and Moses Kuria (Gatundu South).

The legislators, elected on a Jubilee ticket were accused of disrespecting the party leader and publicly calling him out using abusive and derogatory words.
