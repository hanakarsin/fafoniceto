Plans are underway to crown Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i as the Gusii elder and spokesperson by the community's council of elders.

This is after the top elected leaders from the region endorsed the CS for the mantle affirming that he had what it takes to lead the community.

Speaking on Sunday, January 10, after the five-hour closed-door meeting at Kisii Agricultural Training College, the Interior boss said he will first have to seek authority from President Uhuru Kenyatta who elected him to the Cabinet.

“When we asked him if it was in order for him to join us as an elder and also as a community spokesperson, he told us to give him time so that he can consult his boss, President Kenyatta, first,” said James Matundura, the elder's council chairman.

Since Simeone Nyachae's retirement from active politics 10 years ago, the community has not had another spokesman.

Grooming for the top seat

The crowning might be considered as the community's move to prepare Matiang'i for the presidency ahead of the 2022 General Election.

Even though the no-nonsense CS has not publicly announced his political plans, the elders said he has come of age and from his exemplary job, he deserved greater responsibilities including the top seat.

The elder's patron Steven Menge said they implored the CS to accept their proposal to elevate him as a Gusii elder and will set a date for official coronation.

Kisii governor James Ongwae said the community’s unity is paramount and that they will do everything in their power as elected leaders to ensure all leaders speak in one voice.

Senator Sam Ongeri asked those opposed to the idea of crowning the CS to come forward and table their issues.

Talai elders

As previously reported, Deputy President William Ruto was crowned as the Kalenjin political kingpin by some Talai elders who handed him a leadership baton in June, 2020.

The elders also performed traditional rituals to remove bad omen that could hurt the DP’s chances of succeeding President Uhuru Kenyatta.

A section of the elders, however, later dismissed Ruto's coronation as the Kalenjin kingpin and described it as a “fake ceremony”.
