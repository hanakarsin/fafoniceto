Bungoma Governor Wangamati Admits Dinah Nyaboke's Name Was Struck Off Scholarship Beneficiaries' Lis

July 13, 2024

  • Governor Wangamati said his team got unverified information that Dinah Nyaboke's mum was a landlady
  • This led the county officers to delete Nyaboke's name from the list of scholarship beneficiaries
  • Nyaboke was a KCPE 2020 candidate who scored 401 marks; she wanted to go back to primary school and repeat Class Eight due to lack of school fees

Bungoma governor Wycliffe Wangamati has admitted that Dinah Nyaboke, the girl who wanted to repeat Class Eight due to lack of school fees, was removed from the list of county scholarship beneficiaries.

Nyaboke's plight surfaced on Wednesday, July 28, after it emerged that she was about to go back to primary school despite having scored 401 marks in the 2020 Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE).

Speaking to the media on Thursday, July 29, Wangamati said though the student's mother, Priscillah Nyanchera had applied for the scholarship at the county offices, the officers struck off the girl's name from the list of beneficiaries on suspicion her mother was a landlady.

The vetting team visited Nyaboke's family to ascertain whether she was qualified for the scholarship and it would later get unconfirmed reports that Nyanchera was the landlady in the plot where she lives.

"Afterward, someone communicated that Priscillah is the landlady and being a responsible government, we removed her from the scholarship and put her under the special bursary programme," the county boss said.

Wangamati said due to the many cases of false information that the county gets during the application process, they had to remove her name from the scholarship list until the completion of investigations.

However, the governor said he and his team had been able to clarify that Nyanchera is not the landlady and the brilliant girl's name was added to the list of scholarship beneficiaries.

Up until yesterday, Nyokabi, who was among the top KCPE performers at Bungoma D.E.B Primary School and was called to Moi Girls High School in Eldoret, was not sure about joining high school.

Her parents were unable to raise the KSh 53,554 required to enrol her in the school, forcing her to return to her previous primary school to repeat Class Eight.

“I have been selling groceries for a very long time and educating my daughter hoping that in the future she will be able to assist me,” Nyokabi's mother, Priscilla Nyanchera, said.

Sponsorship from leaders

Besides the Bungoma scholarship, several leaders have so far pledged to help Nyaboke as she joins high school.

Lamu governor Fahim Twala, Senator Millicent Omanga, musician Akothee are some of the public figures who have so far announced they will sponsor the Form One student.

Many other Kenyans had also expressed their desire to help Nyaboke.
