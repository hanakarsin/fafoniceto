NCIC summons Kikuyu musician Muigai Wa Njoroge over hard-hitting song scolding Uhuru

April 14, 2024
Post

- Muigai Wa Njoroge's Ino Migunda song rebuked President Uhuru Kenyatta's government largely because of the Kariabangi demolitions and corruption

- The cohesion commission summoned him over the song which it said posed a threat to public harmony

- The musician was asked to appear before the commission on Friday, June 26, failure to which he would be cited for contempt

The National Cohesion and Integration Commission (NCIC) has summoned renowned Kikuyu musician Muigai Wa Njoroge over a controversial song that castigated President Uhuru Kenyatta's government.

The commission said some lyrics in the Ino Miguda song were "insulting" and posed a threat to the harmonious existence of various ethnic communities in Kenya.

In a letter to Njoroge, NCIC asked him to appear at its offices in Upper Hill on Friday, June 26, failure to which he would be charged for contempt.

"Pursuant to section 29 of the NCIC Act states summons issued by the commission must be obeyed," the letter delivered to the Kigutha singer read in part.

Njoroge's song scolded the head of state largely because of the Kariobangi and Ruai demolitions.

"You have demolished houses belonging to the poor in Kariobangi despite the coronavirus pandemic and heavy rainfall. What is that urgency that you want to construct which can't wait? Some deeds are even beyond the devil's imagination!" he sang.

He also called out dynasties for oppressing the poor and in his chorus, he said one day, they would overturn them and divide the land equally.

The musician further called out the government for corruption and questioned how they could afford millions of money for political campaigns but could not provide face masks to Kenyans.

"Flour is priority to the poor as opposed to masks and landlord is knocking at the door for rent while you are enjoying KSh 4 million tea, I'm not sure but coronavirus is here to enrich some people," he said.

This is not the first time the Kikuyu musician is causing a stir after releasing songs targeting politicians.

In 2018, he debuted a new video dubbed, Mbari Ya Kimendero (clan of the greedy) which severely attacked Kenyan leaders describing them as corrupt and greedy.
