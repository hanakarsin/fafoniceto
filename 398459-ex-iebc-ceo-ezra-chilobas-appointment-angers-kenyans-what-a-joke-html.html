A section of Kenyans expressed their disappointment in the appointment of former Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) chief executive officer Ezra Chiloba to the Youth Enterprise Development Fund Board

His appointment was made by Information Communication and Technology (ICT) Cabinet Secretary Joe Mucheru on Thursday, December 31.

Taking their sentiments to social media platforms, Kenyans said the integrity of the former CEO was questionable, adding it was absurd that he had gotten another government job.

Others condemned the "recycling" of former government officials while hundreds of thousands of youths were still languishing in unemployment.

Abu Dawud Okello said:

"We are tired of recycling people, you mean we don't have qualified young men in Kenya. enough is enough Kenyans".

Joh Hovstah said:

"They're slowly revisiting where they had left?"

Caro Aketch said:

"There are many qualified untainted jobless Kenyans and yet they still recycle the likes of Chiloba"

Chiloba is expected to serve in the Youth Enterprise Development Fund Board for three years.

Chiloba's exit from IEBC

The ex-CEO, who was suspended from the electoral agency to pave way for an audit to trace misappropriation of funds in the 2017 General Election, was fired in 2017 from the electoral body.

He later confirmed he had been sacked through a letter signed by IEBC chair Wafula Chebukati saying he was not surprised by the decision.

Chiloba said he was sacked on grounds of missing two summons by a disciplinary committee further citing malice and a conspiracy by Chebukati and other commissioners to have him gone.
