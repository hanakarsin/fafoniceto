Police Officer Sammy Ondimu Excited as Pregnant Woman He Rescued from Streets Gives Birth, Gets Disc

March 16, 2024
Post
  • Police officer Sammy Ondimu had taken Margaret Akinyi to Margaret Kenyatta Hospital mother-baby wing in Nakuru before she went into labour
  • Akinyi, a street woman, gave to a baby girl and named her Talia; and Ondimu, together with other well-wishers organised for a safe place for the new family to stay
  • Ondimu, a member of the National Police Service (NPS), thanked people who gave their donations for Akinyi and even rented her a house

A police officer who has been doing charity work in Nakuru is over the moon after a pregnant street woman he took to the hospital to give birth was discharged following successful delivery.

After getting into labor pain, Sammy Ondimu took Margaret Akinyi to Margaret Kenyatta hospital mother-baby wing Nakuru.

Kind-hearted police

Ondimu said that Akinyi gave birth to a baby girl named Talia, and they were discharged from the hospital on Saturday, September 11.

"Margaret Akinyi and Talia were today afternoon discharged from Margaret Kenyataa hospital mother baby wing Nakuru," Odimu said.

Ondimu divulged that he also mobilised people who helped him raise the money and rented a small house for the Akinyi and her daughter since they did not have a home.

"Thank you so much, friends, who have donated their money and those who shopped for her( Asanteni sanaa marafiki zangu wale wametuma pesa ya shopping, wale wametuma manguo na wale wamelipa) rent like my friend Rani Ramchandani who paid two months rent," he said.

The officer said that immediately after Akinyi gave birth; she wanted to leave the hospital thinking that she was okay.

"This afternoon, I spent my day at PGH talking to mama Talia (Maggy Akinyi). She delivered through Cesarean section (CS ), and she wanted to leave the hospital because she did not understand what was going on," he said.

At the same time, he said that he did not pay a lot of money for the hospital bill saying Linda mama cover really helped out.

"Finally to the staffer Margaret Kenyatta marternity wing you are very important people. Linda mama cover is the best now i will not have to pay a lot of money for bills just few coins for antibiotics ," he said.

The cop is known for his good works and generosity, and he is also an advocate for 'Polisi ni Binadamu.'

Romantic police

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that the humble cop flew his wife to a special getaway in Malindi during her birthday and 17th marriage anniversary celebration.

Ondimu pampered his better half with love on her birthday, and he said she was a supportive, prayerful, and Godfearing woman.

The loveable cop also noted that their marriage hasn't been a smooth journey, and he thanked her for being understanding.
