MPs, private schools accuse education ministry of withholding student's fees

March 14, 2024
Post

- The Private Schools Association chairman appeared before lawmakers and launched the complain

- The association claimed the fee of about 25, 000 students who transferred from public to private schools was still being held

- National Assembly's committee on education asked education CS George Magoha to appear before the MPs and shed more light on the matter

Members of Parliament have accused education ministry of using National Education Management Information System (NEMIS) to fraudulently hold fees meant for capitation of students transferred to private schools.

This came after the Kenya Private School Association (KPSA) Chair Mutheu Kasanga appeared before the National Assembly's standing committee on education and launched the complain.

Kasanga claimed about 25,000 students had transferred from public to private schools since the free education program was launched but the ministry was still holding their fees.

‘’We are reading mischief in the ministry for its supposed to allocate fees to private schools as it does in all private universities,’’ he said on Tuesday.

According to the governments free secondary education program, each student in boarding schools is allocated KSh 22, 500 annually.

The committee blamed education CS George Magoha and respective public schools for holding data and benefits of students who transferred to private secondary schools without any explanation.

Committee chair Patrick Injendi and his vice Amos Kimunya asked Magoha to come clean on the damning allegations noting they were tainting the reputation of the ministry and government at large.

Ijendi said the CS should appear before the committee to shed more light on the matter.
