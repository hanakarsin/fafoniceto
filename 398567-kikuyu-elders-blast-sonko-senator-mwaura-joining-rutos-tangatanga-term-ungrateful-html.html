Kikuyu elders have blasted former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko and Nominated Senator Isaac Mwaura for dumping President Uhuru Kenyatta and joining forces with the hustler nation.

The elders termed the duo as ungrateful while also calling on Mt Kenya politicians to support the president and not leave him alone.

Led by the council’s national chairman, Wachira Kiago, Ndaragwa MP Jeremiah Kioni and Laikipia Governor Ndiritu Muriithi, the elders said all politicians should respect the head of state and avoid insulting him, Daily Nation reported.

"I ask all leaders from our community who have defected from Jubilee to come back home because you will talk ill of the president and he will still remain the best president that Kenya has had," Kiago said.

The elders warned Sonko and Mwaura that their political careers were headed for a major crash for abandoning Uhuru.

They also drummed support for the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) and urged all leaders to rally behind the document for the interests Kenyans.

Travelled to Nanyuki

The Kikuyu elders said this after holding prayers for the first in command and the country at large.

They travelled from Nairobi to Nanyuki via a train and held the prayers while facing Mt Kenya.

The new Tangatanga members threw their weight behind Deputy President William Ruto on Thursday, December 31, during the homecoming ceremony of Msambweni MP Feisar Badal.

Sonko, in his speech, said Uhuru was a dictator and blamed him for his impeachment while the senator said the Kenyattas have been in power for too long.

"The Kenyatta family has ruled Kenya for 15 years and 10 years (of Uhuru's regime) are also coming to an end soon. We say that is enough, now let's move forward and see what we can do as youths of this nation," he said.
