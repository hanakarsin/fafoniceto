Have you been wondering how to get a Certificate of Good Conduct on eCitizen? Today, you can apply for this annually renewable document provided by the Criminal Investigations Department (C.I.D) at the comfort of your home. Thanks to the digital revolution, Kenyans can now access government services without visiting any office.

eCitizen is a platform that eCitizen that offers access to information and services provided by the Kenyan Government. To join the portal, you can visit the website and follow the quick steps to access the available services. Here is how to get a Certificate of Good conduct on eCitizen.

How to get a Certificate of Good Conduct in Kenya

The certificate of good conduct is also known as a Police Clearance Certificate. Various instances require you to have this document, for example, when applying for a Public Service Vehicle badge or vying for a government post. The paper is proof that you are a morally abiding citizen who follows the law.

Most potential employers also require that their prospective employees provide a copy of the police clearance certificate. Company directors are also required to produce the certificate as a pre-qualification for providing tender services. The following categories of people are eligible for the Kenya Police Certificate of Good Conduct application:

  • Kenyan citizens within the country or abroad
  • Foreign citizens
  • Aliens and refugees in Kenya

How to apply for a certificate of good conduct Kenya online

The primary purpose of a Good Conduct certificate is to validate that you have a clean criminal record. Previously, only citizens above 18 years could apply for the document. However, those below that age can now obtain police clearance if they produce an original copy of their birth certificate and can apply through their parent's or guardian's eCitizen account.

Applicants need to produce a national identification card alongside a clear copy of the same. Additionally, you need to have an eCitizen account, which you can create through the website. Here are the steps to follow when applying for a Certificate of Good Conduct Kenya online:

  • Log in to your eCitizen account and sign in using your username (your email address) and password, redirecting you to the eCitizen dashboard.
  • Click "Get Service Now," which is under the category "Directorate of Criminal Investigations." Follow the application guidelines.
  • Choose the payment mode to pay Ksh. 1,050 via M-Pesa for the Good Conduct Certificate to be processed.
  • Download certificate of good conduct and print two copies of the payment invoice and a copy of the C24 form on both sides of the A4 paper.
  • Proceed to present the document to the nearest CID offices once you complete the online application. You are required to carry the invoice, C24 document, and an original identification document or original birth certificate in the case of a minor. C.I.D personnel will take you through fingerprint processing at the divisional CID offices or a Huduma Centre near you.

    If you are a foreigner, you can apply for police clearance if you have been a resident for more than six months. You will also need to produce a document from your employer or training institution and valid work permits. In addition, students must have pupils' passes accompanied with relevant testimonials proving the completion of the course and other relevant documents.

    In addition, non-citizens should also provide a letter stipulating the reason for the certificate and the original copies of identification documents. They will also pay a fee of KShs 1,000.

    A refugee applying for police clearance must present themselves with their original and copy of UNHCR valid mandate letters. The movement pass from transit centres or refugee camps is also a mandatory requirement. Unfortunately, recommendations from other governments or NGOs are not accepted. Refugees will also pay KShs 1,000.

    Police clearance certificate validity

    The certificate of good conduct Kenya is valid for one year from the date of printing, which means that you have to renew it annually. The primary reason for this is to synchronize the Huduma Centre certificate of good conduct with any criminal records entered into the police books within the period. You can also renew the document through the eCitizen account.

    Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

    Here are some of the frequently asked questions by Kenyans wishing to apply for the eCitizen Good Conduct Certificate:

    1. Can I complete the clearance using the eCitizen good conduct download?

    eCitizen facilitates the application process halfway, including filling or printing the C24 form. However, you must visit your nearest Huduma Center, any DCI office in Kenya, or the Kenya Embassy if you are abroad for your fingerprints to be taken.

    2. How long does a Certificate of Good Conduct take to process?

    After you finish your Good Conduct application process, there is a waiting period of two weeks to one month. You can then collect it at the DCI offices or your nearest Huduma Centre.
