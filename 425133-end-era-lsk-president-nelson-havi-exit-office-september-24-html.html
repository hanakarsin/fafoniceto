End of Era: LSK President Nelson Havi to Exit Office on September 24

June 06, 2024
Post
  • Nelson Havi said that on that day, he will make his last contribution to the society
  • Havi also asserted that on the same day they will have an SGM where LSK will constitute the elections board ahead of the election on March 17, 2022
  • Havi noted that he was thrilled that through his leadership, LSK accomplished several things, citing stopping the BBI

President for the Law Society of Kenya (LSK) Nelson Havi has announced he will vacating office before the end of this month.

On Monday, September 6, Havi said that September 24, 2021, will be his last day in the office. This will mark the end of his 18-months stint at the society.

Havi said that on the same day of his exit, a Special General Meeting (SGM) has been scheduled.

"September 24, 2021, SGM will be my last assignment as LSK president," he said.

Havi's achievements

The LSK president asserted that during the SGM, LSK will constitute the elections board and approve the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) to conduct the polls on March 17, 2022.

Havi, who is aspiring to join politics, said that the LSK accomplished several things through his leadership, a major one being stopping the Building Bridges Initiative.

"With over 20 judgments against government, advisory for dissolution of parliament and stop of BBI, we have done enough," he stated while listing some of his achievements while at the helm at LSK.

Although his term ends on March 24, 2022, Havi intimated that he and the outgoing council members would not take up any duties on behalf of LSK after September 24.

"We will not take up any cases that involve public interest nor be involved in any litigations. We are not going to submit any memoranda in parliament," Havi stated.

Havi was elected as LSK president in February 2020, and his two-year term was set to end in February 2022.

Wrangles at LSK

In a separate story, TUKO.co.ke reported that Havi secured a cash bail after he was arrested on Tuesday, July 13, following claims of physically assaulting suspended LSK CEO Mercy Wambua.

The vocal lawyer was nabbed at his office in Nairobi and taken to the DCI offices for interrogation.
