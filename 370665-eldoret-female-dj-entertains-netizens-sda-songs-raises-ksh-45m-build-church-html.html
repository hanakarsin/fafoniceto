Eldoret female DJ entertains netizens with SDA songs, raises KSh 4.5M to build church

May 06, 2024
Post

- Kezia Cherono started live streaming SDA music on her social media platforms and managed to raise KSh 4.5 million while at it

- Cherono said the coronavirus period had been a blessing to her since she would never have managed to raise that kind of money to build her home church

- Besides her remarkable work in raising the funds, Cherono is also reportedly the first woman to have a successful career as a DJ in Marakwet

They say every cloud has a silver lining, but not many can find the silver lining in the coronavirus pandemic being that this season has been so difficult.

However, an Eldoret based female DJ proved that it is possible to see opportunities even during the pandemic after she successfully raised more than KSh 4.5 million to build a home church in Kapsowar, Elgeyo-Marakwet county.

Speaking to Citizen TV, Keziah Cherono said she started doing Seventh Adventist Church (SDA) songs during the coronavirus period and would live-stream her performance online to entertain Kenyans.

During the live streams, admins reached out to her and asked her how they could help and she quickly said she wanted to raise funds during her performances to build an SDA church at home.

"I have been playing in clubs before, for corporate events but this is the first time I have played SDA music during corona season.""Admins reached out to me and asked what they can do to help me and I asked them to help raise funds to build my home church at home," the DJ said.

Despite losing her job, Cherono said COVID-19 pandemic had been a blessing to her because she would never have managed to raise the money to build her local SDA home church were it not for it.

"It has taken us more than 40 days to ever build a church. The most we have done is made of an iron sheet made structure," she said.

The construction of the church is underway and one of the church members said not even in their wildest dreams had they ever thought they would build a chapel much less in this tumultuous year.

On her social media platforms, Cherono also started a fund-drive to help DJ Evolve who was shot in the neck by MP Babu Owino on January 17.

"Today we do it for DJ Evolve, a colleague and a brother. And as we pray for his healing, his peace and strength for his family, whatever contribution we get we will send to his family to ease their burden!" she wrote on July 25.

Besides doing a phenomenal job in her efforts to raise the funds, Cherone also challenged the status quo by becoming the first woman in Marakwet to have a successful career as a DJ.

"In the Marakwet community, women are not allowed to do a lot. They are not allowed to speak in front of men or do big projects," she said.
