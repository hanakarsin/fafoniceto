Kenyans concerned COVID-19 vaccines could disappear like Jack Ma's donations: &quot;It's the same gov't&quot;

April 10, 2024
Post

- Government officials led by Health CS Kagwe received the first batch of COVID-19 vaccines and they pledged to take care of the jabs

- However, Kenyans did not believe a word they said going by the concerns that some of them raised on social media

- A section of Kenyans felt since the same government was accused of misappropriating Jack Ma's donations, the arrival of COVID-19 vaccines could be the beginning of another scandal

Kenyans have conveyed their concerns about the safety of COVID-19 vaccines which landed at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) a few hours ago.

Though Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe assured Kenyans that the 1.02 million doses of AstraZeneca-Oxford vaccine would be accounted for, many did not seem to believe him.

Kenyans on Twitter wondered how they could entrust the same government which mismanaged Jack Ma's donations to ensure the vaccines would be in safe hands.

Narok senator Ledama Ole Kina waded into the debate and said it was without a doubt that a vaccines scandal was imminent because "this is Kenya".

"Vaccines scandals loading! There is no way this will pass without one! This is Kenya! We always find away to mess things up! Sounds perfect on paper until the implementation part comes," he said.

Gicheru said it was interesting that the same government officers that went to receive the new jabs were the same officials who picked the Jack Ma donations which were later sold to Tanzania, allegedly.

"So the same government officials who went to JKIA to receive Jack Ma's donation which later found their way to Tanzania went to receive the Covid-19 Vaccine? Just remember from Kitengela, Namanga is not far," he said.

Omogambi said:

"You are all wondering why the COVID-19 Vaccines have not disappeared like Jack Ma's masks? This is because CSs, PSs and politicians among the first people to be vaccinated."

A damning investigative report in August 2020 exposed the rot at the Kenya Medical Supplies Authority (KEMSA) and how the government misappropriated COVID-19 donations.

The report revealed that over a dozen unscrupulous Tanzanian companies benefited from Jack Ma's donations, which included 100,000 masks and 20,000 testing kits.

However, Kagwe denied the allegations and maintained none of the COVID-19 donations or funds were mismanaged.
