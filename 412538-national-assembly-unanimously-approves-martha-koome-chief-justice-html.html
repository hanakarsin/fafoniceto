The National Assembly has adopted the Justice and Legal Affairs Committee report on lady justice Martha Koome's nomination as Kenya's chief justice.

The committee co-chairperson Muturi Kigano moved and tabled the motion on the approval of Koome's appointment on Wednesday, May 19, following a rigorous vetting one week ago.

Kigano told the House that the committee had considered all the questions raised against Koome and unanimously endorsed her after carefully auditing each concern vis-a-viz her suitability.

The nominee had shared her vision to ensure wide access to justice which she demonstrated at a previous station in Kitale when she extended judicial services and justice to far-flung areas.

"We found that she's able to think outside the box, and she would not be living in a judicial island. She aspires to do substantial justice other than delving on technical formalities," Kigano added.

The parliament will now forward her name to President Uhuru Kenyatta for a formal appointment and swearing-in at the State House.

The Court of Appeal judge, who emerged victorious among nine other applicants, will be the first female to sit at the helm of the Judiciary.

Other candidates interviewed for the position were Justice Said Juma Chitembwe, Professor Patricia Mbote, Philip Murgor, Lawyer Fredrick Ngatia, Justice Marete Njagi, Justice Matthews Nduma Nderi, Justice William Ouko, Moni Wekesa and Alice Yano.

During the interviews, the lady justice told the panel that she earned a bachelor's degree in 1985 and a master’s in Public International Law in 2010 from the University of London.

“I grew up in rural areas in Meru in a village, so I am a villager to the truest sense. My parents were peasant farmers, and we were 18 children from two mothers," she stated.
