President Uhuru Kenyatta has appointed Kenya Medical Supplies Authority (KEMSA) chairman Kembi Gitura to the board of Communication Authority of Kenya (CA).

Gitura will serve as the head of the CA board for a period of three years effective April 18, 2021.

KEMSA scandal

Gitura, who is also the immediate former Murang’a senator, has been at the helm of KEMSA board for a period of two years which was marred with corruption allegations.

His appointment comes amid a probe into a scandal involving the award of tenders to supply Personal Protective Equipment (PPEs) at KEMSA

Eight companies implicated in the KSh 7.8 billion scandal were summoned by the National Assembly Public Investment Committee.

Kenya lost KSh 2.3 billion

The Senate Joint Committee on Health and COVID-19 Response on Wednesday, September 30, 2020, received a preliminary report into the KEMSA shady procurement.

The report was presented before the committee by Auditor-General Nancy Gathangu.

Gathangu told the committee Kenya lost KSh 2.3 billion as a result of irregular and unprocedural tendering process in the wake of the coronavirus pandemic.

The KSh 2.3 billion loss, she explained, was a result of over-procurement which saw Kenya purchase COVID-19 medical supplies worth KSh 6.3 billion instead of KSh 4 billion.

As previously reported, Gitura dismissed corruption allegations at KEMSA saying it was a mere sensational report being propagated by the media without facts.
