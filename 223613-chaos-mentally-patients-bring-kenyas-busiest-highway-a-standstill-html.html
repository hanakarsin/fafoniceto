Chaos as mentally ill patients bring Kenya's busiest highway to a standstill

April 09, 2024
Post

- Road users on Thika road were stuck in traffic for hours after patients escaped Mathari hospital

- Over 100 mental patients escaped the hospital as doctors and nurses kick off nationwide strike

- A major manhunt has been launched in search of the mental patients

There was drama along Thika Road today, December 5 after mental patients escaped from Mathari Hospital in Nairobi.

A major operation has been launched in search of over 100 mental patients who have escaped in the ongoing doctors and nurses' strike.

Motorists and road users were forced to wait in traffic after the patients escaped from Mathari Hospital located along Thika road.

Health Cabinet Secretary Cleopa Mailu said the strike notice was premature since the court had given the doctor's and nurses 90 days a court had given medics to negotiate with the government.

Mailu pleaded with the health workers to report to work as they are willing to negotiate to prevent a crisis and loss of lives.
